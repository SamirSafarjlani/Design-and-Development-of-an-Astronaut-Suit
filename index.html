// 01 — ABOUT THE PROJECT

Next-Generation EVA Suit

This capstone project focuses on designing and developing a next-generation astronaut suit for extravehicular activities (EVA). The suit improves astronaut safety, mobility, situational awareness, and survivability in extreme environments through integrated electronics, embedded systems, and mechanical design.

Engineered at the University of Technology Sydney, this project bridges aerospace engineering, embedded systems, mechanical design, and human factors to produce a comprehensive life support and mobility platform for future space missions.

// 02 — SUIT OVERVIEW

Full Suit Architecture

Helmet HUD
Head-Up Display System

The helmet-integrated Head-Up Display provides astronauts with real-time critical data overlaid on their field of view, eliminating the need to look away from the task at hand. The transparent OLED display projects telemetry directly onto the visor surface.

  • Heart rate monitoring (BPM)
  • SpO₂ blood oxygen saturation
  • Internal and external temperature
  • Remaining oxygen levels
  • CO₂ concentration alerts
  • Suit pressure warnings
  • Battery level indicator
PLSS — Portable Life Support System
Primary Life Support Backpack

The PLSS is the backbone of astronaut survivability. This integrated backpack system manages all life-critical functions including oxygen supply, CO₂ scrubbing, temperature control, and emergency reserves throughout the entire EVA duration.

  • Primary oxygen supply tanks
  • CO₂ removal via LiOH scrubbers
  • Active thermal regulation loops
  • Forced air circulation fans
  • High-density battery pack
  • Emergency backup O₂ system
Exoskeleton Gloves
Assisted Dexterity System

Traditional EVA gloves reduce grip strength by up to 60%. Our exoskeleton glove system uses servo-assisted mechanisms and flex sensors to amplify grip strength, reduce fatigue, and maintain precision during critical tool operations.

  • Servo-assisted grip amplification
  • Flex sensor array (per finger)
  • Reduced hand fatigue by ~70%
  • Tactile feedback preservation
  • Tool-lock precision assist
  • Thermal hand warming elements
Suit Body
Structural & Pressure Shell

The primary suit body serves as the pressure barrier between the astronaut and the vacuum of space. Multiple layers of advanced materials work together to maintain internal pressure, thermal control, and protection from micrometeorite impacts.

  • Multi-layer pressure retention
  • Thermal micro-meteorite shield
  • Lightweight titanium structural joints
  • Embedded cable routing channels
  • Modular assembly connectors
  • Radiation shielding layer
Chest Control Interface
Primary Mission Control Panel

The chest-mounted interface provides the astronaut with direct tactile access to all critical suit systems without requiring fine dexterity. Large, glove-compatible controls and a bright OLED display allow rapid system monitoring and adjustment.

  • Central OLED status display
  • Oxygen flow rate control
  • Temperature setpoint adjustment
  • Emergency system toggles
  • Communication system controls
  • Battery status indicators

// 05 — MATERIALS & ENGINEERING

Suit Construction

The suit body integrates multiple material layers, each serving a critical function. The design prioritizes minimizing mass while maximizing protection, thermal performance, and structural integrity.

🛡️

Outer Protective Shell

High-durability Ortho-Fabric outer layer resistant to abrasion, micrometeorite impact, and UV radiation. Provides the first line of defense against the space environment.

🌡️

Thermal Insulation

Multi-layer insulation (MLI) with aluminized Mylar and Dacron spacer layers. Maintains internal temperature across the extreme range of −160°C to +120°C.

💨

Pressure Retention

Neoprene-coated nylon bladder layer maintains the pressurized atmosphere essential for astronaut survival, engineered for zero-leak performance under cyclic loading.

🔩

Structural Supports

Lightweight titanium alloy bearings and carbon fiber reinforced polymer joints provide the structural rigidity required at pressure joints while minimizing added mass.

⚡

Embedded Electronics

Flexible PCB traces routed through dedicated channels within suit layers. All electronics are radiation-hardened and sealed against outgassing in vacuum.

⚖️

Weight Optimization

Every component selected through mass-budget analysis. Target suit weight under 40 kg dry mass (excluding PLSS) through strategic use of advanced composites and additive manufacturing.

// 08 — IMPACT & APPLICATIONS

Beyond the Mission

🌍

Social Impact

This project directly improves human safety in one of the most extreme environments ever encountered. By advancing EVA suit technology, we reduce risk for astronauts who push the boundary of human knowledge and exploration.

The real-time health monitoring platform developed here has direct applications in emergency response, industrial safety, and remote healthcare.

♻️

Sustainability

The PLSS design optimizes resource consumption throughout extended EVA missions. Smart power management, efficient CO₂ scrubbing, and precise oxygen regulation minimize waste and extend mission duration without increasing consumable mass.

Lower mass means lower launch costs, directly reducing the carbon footprint of each mission.

🔭

Technology Transfer

Technologies developed for this suit have broad applicability across high-risk industries and humanitarian sectors.

🔥 Firefighting ⛏️ Mining ☢️ Hazmat Response 🏥 Medical Wearables 🛡️ Defense 🏗️ Construction
