// 01 — ABOUT THE PROJECT

Next-Generation EVA Suit

This capstone project focuses on designing and developing a next-generation astronaut suit for extravehicular activities (EVA). The suit improves astronaut safety, mobility, situational awareness, and survivability in extreme environments through integrated electronics, embedded systems, and mechanical design.

Engineered at the University of Technology Sydney, this project bridges aerospace engineering, embedded systems, mechanical design, and human factors to produce a comprehensive life support and mobility platform for future space missions.

// 06 — IMPACT & APPLICATIONS

Beyond the Mission

🌍

Social Impact

This project directly improves human safety in one of the most extreme environments ever encountered. By advancing EVA suit technology, we reduce risk for astronauts who push the boundary of human knowledge and exploration.

The real-time health monitoring platform developed here has direct applications in emergency response, industrial safety, and remote healthcare.

♻️

Sustainability

The PLSS design optimizes resource consumption throughout extended EVA missions. Smart power management, efficient CO₂ scrubbing, and precise oxygen regulation minimize waste and extend mission duration without increasing consumable mass.

Lower mass means lower launch costs, directly reducing the carbon footprint of each mission.

🔭

Technology Transfer

Technologies developed for this suit have broad applicability across high-risk industries and humanitarian sectors.

🔥 Firefighting ⛏️ Mining ☢️ Hazmat Response 🏥 Medical Wearables 🛡️ Defense 🏗️ Construction
